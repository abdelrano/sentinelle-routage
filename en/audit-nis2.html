Why audit your routing now?

NIS2 asks for evidence, not intentions. The NIS2 directive and implementing regulation 2024/2690 require essential and important entities in the digital-infrastructure sector to run documented risk-management measures. Prefix hijacks and expired ROAs are a very real risk (MyEtherWallet 2018, Verizon 2019, Celer 2022), and a dated, signed routing assessment is exactly the kind of artefact an auditor files. We say NIS2 readiness deliberately: the audit doesn't replace your compliance programme, it supplies its routing chapter — the one generalist security firms don't cover.

Non-intrusive by design. The audit relies exclusively on public sources: RIPE NCC RIS collectors, RPKI validation (local Routinator instance), IRR route objects (RADB), the RIPE database. Nothing to install, no access to your routers or IT systems, no clearance process on your side. You can order the audit without opening an access request with your IT department.

Frequently asked questions


Who performs the audit?

The network engineer who built Sentinelle Routage: 18 years on critical networks, MPLS backbone (Juniper, Cisco, Nokia). The public checker has already analysed 450+ ASNs, and the methodology was covered in a blog post by Stéphane Bortzmeyer (AFNIC).

Is this a NIS2 compliance audit?

No — and be wary of anyone selling you one while national transpositions are still settling. This is a technical audit of the routing chapter, formatted to feed your NIS2 readiness file (with an Article 21 mapping in the Pack). It complements an organisational audit; it doesn't replace it.

Do you need access to our network?

No. Everything is done from public data (RIS, RPKI, IRR, RIPE DB). That's what makes the audit fast and risk-free for you: nothing to install, nothing to open up.

What if our routing is already clean?

Great: the report documents it in black and white, with a date and a method. That's precisely what an auditor or an enterprise customer will ask you to prove. And if we find blind spots (permissive ROAs, prefixes without IRR objects, partial visibility), you leave with the plan to fix them.
